HANDBALL

Rules, Player Positions, Techniques and Game Fundamentals

Handball is a fast-paced team sport played by two teams of seven players. Success depends on teamwork, technical skills, tactical awareness and physical fitness.

HANDBALL RULES

Match Structure

A standard match consists of two 30-minute halves with a halftime break of 10–15 minutes. Each team has six field players and one goalkeeper.

Player Movement

Players may take up to three steps while holding the ball and can hold it for a maximum of three seconds.

Fouls & Violations

Dangerous contact, tripping, pulling or hitting opponents is prohibited and may result in penalties.

Scoring

A goal is awarded when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the posts.

PLAYER POSITIONS

Goalkeeper

The last line of defense. Uses quick reflexes and agility to stop shots and start counterattacks.

Backcourt Players

Left back, center back and right back organize attacks, shoot from distance and support defense.

Wings

Fast players positioned near the sidelines who specialize in fast breaks and scoring from difficult angles.

Pivot

Operates near the goal area, creating space and opportunities while dealing with defensive pressure.

KEY TECHNIQUES

Movement & Positioning

Proper positioning improves both offensive and defensive effectiveness.

Agility & Footwork

Quick movements and balance help players react efficiently during gameplay.

Passing Skills

Accurate passing and fast transitions are essential for maintaining possession.

Pattern Recognition

Players learn to anticipate movements and recognize tactical opportunities.

GAME FUNDAMENTALS

Physical Demands

Handball requires speed, endurance, strength and explosive movements.

Technical Skills

Players must master passing, shooting, defending and ball control.

Formations

Teams use structured formations such as 6-0 or 3-2-1 to organize defense and attack.

Tactics

Successful teams adapt strategies depending on opponents and match situations.
